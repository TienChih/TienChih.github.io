Max/Min

Given a function \(f(x)\) we say \(a\) is a global maximum if \(f(x)\) is \(f(a)\geq f(c)\) for every \(c\) in the domain of \(x\).

Given a function \(f(x)\) we say \(a\) is a local maximum if in a neighborhood of \(a\) we have \(f(x)\) is \(f(a)\geq f(c)\) for every \(c\) in the neighborhood.

Criteria

Let \(f(x)\) be a differentiable function. Suppose \(f(x)\) has a local maximum when \(x=a\).

  • What can be said about \(f'(a)\)?
  • What can be said about \(f'(c)\) when \(c\) is just below \(a\)?
  • What can be said about \(f'(c)\) when \(c\) is just above \(a\)?
