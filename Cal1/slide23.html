u-Substitution

Recall that \(\frac{d}{dx}[u]=u'\frac{du}{dx}\)

Given an integrable function \(g(x)\), we have that \[\int g(u)u'dx = \int g(u)\frac{du}{dx}dx = \int g(u) du=G(u)+C\] where \(G\) is an anti-derivative of \(g\).

Consider \[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx.\]

Let \(u=\ln(x)+1\). Then \(u'=\frac{1}{x}\).

Recall that \(u'dx=du\). so \(\frac{1}{x}dx=du\).

Consider \[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx.\]

Let \(u=\ln(x)+1\). Then \(u'=\frac{1}{x}\).

Recall that \(u'dx=du\). so \(\frac{1}{x}dx=du\).

\[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx=\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{u} dx\]

Consider \[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx.\]

Let \(u=\ln(x)+1\). Then \(u'=\frac{1}{x}\).

Recall that \(u'dx=du\). so \(\frac{1}{x}dx=du\).

\[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx=\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{u} dx=\int \sqrt{u} du\]

Consider \[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx.\]

Let \(u=\ln(x)+1\). Then \(u'=\frac{1}{x}\).

Recall that \(u'dx=du\). so \(\frac{1}{x}dx=du\).

\[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx=\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{u} dx=\int \sqrt{u} du=\frac{2}{3}u^\frac{3}{2}+C\]

Consider \[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx.\]

Let \(u=\ln(x)+1\). Then \(u'=\frac{1}{x}\).

Recall that \(u'dx=du\). so \(\frac{1}{x}dx=du\).

\[\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{\ln(x)+1} dx=\int \frac{1}{x} \sqrt{u} dx=\int \sqrt{u} du=\frac{2}{3}u^\frac{3}{2}+C=\frac{2}{3}(\ln(x)+1)^\frac{3}{2}+C.\]

Consider \[\int_0^4 (t+1)e^{t^2+2t} dt.\]

Why isn't \[\int_0^4 (t+1)e^{t^2+2t} dt= \int_0^4 \frac{1}{2}e^udu ?\]

Consider \[\int_0^4 (t+1)e^{t^2+2t} dt.\]

\[\int_0^4 (t+1)e^{t^2+2t} dt = \int_{t=0}^{t=4} \frac{1}{2}e^udu = \int_{u=?}^{u=?} \frac{1}{2}e^udu.\] What are the \(u\) bounds of the last integral? Can you evaluate it?

Also note: \[\int_0^4(t+1)e^{t^2+2t} dt= \int_{t=0}^{t=4} \frac{1}{2}e^udu = \int_{u=?}^{u=?} \frac{1}{2}e^udu = \frac{1}{2}e^u|_{u=?}^{u=?}=\frac{1}{2}e^{t^2+2t}|_{t=0}^{t=4}.\] Can you evaluate it?
