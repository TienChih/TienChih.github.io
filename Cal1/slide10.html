The Chain Rule

If \(g(x)\) is differentiable, and \(f(x)\) is differentiable at \(g(x)\), then: \[\frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]=f'(g(x))g'(x).\] Alternatively, if \(y=f(u), u=g(x)\) both differentiable, then \[\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx}.\]

Naieve "proof" of The Chain Rule

\[\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]\\ \end{array}\]

Naieve "proof" of The Chain Rule

\[\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]&=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\\ \end{array}\]

Naieve "proof" of The Chain Rule

\[\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]&=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\\ \end{array}\]

Naieve "proof" of The Chain Rule

\[\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]&=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ \end{array}\]

Naieve "proof" of The Chain Rule

\[\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]&=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ &=&\left(\displaystyle\lim_{\Delta g\to 0}\frac{f(g(x)+\Delta g)-f(g(x))}{\Delta g} \right)\left(\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\right)\\ \end{array}\]

Naieve "proof" of The Chain Rule

\[\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))]&=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{h}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\\ &=&\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(g(x+h))-f(g(x))}{g(x+h)-g(x)}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\\ &=&\left(\displaystyle\lim_{\Delta g\to 0}\frac{f(g(x)+\Delta g)-f(g(x))}{\Delta g} \right)\left(\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}\right)\\ &=&f'(g(x))g'(x)\\ \end{array}\]
