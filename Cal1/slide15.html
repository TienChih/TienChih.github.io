Question:
Suppose a car that is equipped with an E-Z Pass drives from the toll plaza in Carlisle, PA (milage marker 226 on the PA Turnpike) to the one in Valley Forge (marker 326) in 1 hour and 15 minutes. A few days later the driver receives a speeding ticket in the mail. How did the PA state troopers know that the driver was speeding?

What was the average velocity of the car during this 1.25 hour trip?

  • Let \(d(t)\) denote the mileage marker after \(t\) hours.
  • \(d(0)=226, d(1.25)=326\).
  • \[a_{vg}=\frac{d(1.25)-d(0)}{1.25-0}=\frac{326-226}{1.25}=80\ \text{mph}.\]

Question:
Suppose a car that is equipped with an E-Z Pass drives from the toll plaza in Carlisle, PA (milage marker 226 on the PA Turnpike) to the one in Valley Forge (marker 326) in 1 hour and 15 minutes. A few days later the driver receives a speeding ticket in the mail. How did the PA state troopers know that the driver was speeding?

  • Case 1: The car's initial velocity is below 80 mph. (\(d'(0)< 80\))
  • Case 2: The car's initial velocity is greater 80 mph. (\(d'(0)> 80\))
  • Case 3: The car's initial velocity is exactly 80 mph. (\(d'(0)= 80\))

There is some point (at least one) during this journey when the velocity is exactly 80mph. (we don't know where!)

There is a \(c\) in \([0, 1.25]\) so that: \[d'(c)=80=\frac{d(1.25)-d(0)}{1.25-0}\]

Let \(f(x)\) be continuous on \([a,b]\) be differentiable on \((a,b)\), with average rate of change over this interval \[m=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.\]



Claim:
There is a \(c\) in \([a,b]\) so that \[f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.\]

Case 1

Let \(\color{red}{f(x)}\) be defined on \([a,b]\) and \(\displaystyle f'(a)< \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.\)

Is there a \(\color{blue}c\) in \([a,b]\) so that \[\color{blue}{f'(c)}=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}?\]

Case 2

Let \(\color{red}{f(x)}\) be defined on \([a,b]\) and \(\displaystyle f'(a)> \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.\)

Is there a \(\color{blue}c\) in \([a,b]\) so that \[\color{blue}{f'(c)}=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}?\]

Case 3

\(f'(a)=\displaystyle \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.\)
Is there a \(c\) in \([a,b]\) so that \(f'(c)= \displaystyle \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}?\)

Let \(c=a\).

Justification

  • Let \(m=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\), and \(h(x)=f(x)-m(x-a)\).
  • Since \(h(a)=h(b)\), there is a \(c\) in \([a,b]\) so that \(h'(c)=0\). (Rolle's Theorem: What goes up must come down!)
  • \(h'(x)=f'(x)-m\).
  • \(0=h'(c)=f'(c)-m\) and\(f'(c)=m\)!
