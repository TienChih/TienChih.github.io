The Fence

Suppose a farmer has 300 feet of fencing to build a rectangular fence against a river. What is the largest area it could enclose, and what would be it's dimensions?

\(A=xy, 300=2x+y\). Can I express \(A\) in terms of just \(x\) or just \(y\)? Can I find \(A\)'s extrema?'

The Can

A cylindrical can is meant to hold 1 liter (1000 cm\(^3\)) of oil. What dimensions (\(r, h\) in cm) would minimize the amount of material needed.

Recall for a cylinder, the volume is \(V=\pi r^2h\) and the surface area is \(A=2\pi rh+2\pi r^2\).

Use \(1000=\pi r^2h\) to solve for \(h\). (Trust me this is better than solving for \(r\)).

Minimize \(A(r)=\text{somthing }r\).

For the \(r\) you found, what are \(A, h\)?

The Doggy

A doggy can run at 40 feet per second and swim at 4 feet per second. A child is drowning in a lake 20 feet from shore, 80 feet down the shoreline. What path should the doggy take to minimize the time it'll take them to reach drowning child?

Let \(x\) denote how far the doggy runs in feet before jumping into the water (point \(P\)).

  • Given any choice \(x\) in \([0,80]\), how far is \(P\) from the child? Call this \(s\).
  • Given any choice \(x\) in \([0,80]\) long does it take for the doggy to run \(x\) feet?
  • Given any choice \(x\) in \([0,80]\) long does it take for the doggy to swim \(s\) feet?
  • Given any choice \(x\) find \(T(x)\), the time it will take the doggy to reach the child.
  • Minimize \(T(x)\).
