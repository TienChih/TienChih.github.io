Given a function \(Q(t)\), the average rate of change of \(Q(t)\) over \([c,t]\) is \[m_{PQ} = \frac{Q(t)-Q(c)}{t-c}\] which is also the slope of the secant line between points \(P=(t, Q(t))\) and \(Q=(c, Q(c))\).

For \(t_0 < t_1 < t_2\) we have \(R_1=\frac{Q(t_1)-Q(t_0)}{t_1-t_0}={\Delta Q_1 \over \Delta t_1}, R_2=\frac{Q(t_2)-Q(t_1)}{t_2-t_1}={\Delta Q_2 \over \Delta t_2}\) and \[R={\Delta Q \over \Delta t} = \frac{Q(t_2)-Q(t_0)}{t_2-t_0} = \frac{\Delta t_1 R_1 - \Delta t_2 R_2}{\Delta t_1 + \Delta t_2}.\]

\[\min(R_1, R_2)\leq R \leq \max(R_1, R_2).\]

\[\min(R_1, R_2, \ldots, R_n)\leq R \leq \max(R_1, R_2, \ldots, R_n).\]

Consider a planet with gravitational potential \(U(r)=-\frac{G}{r}\), \(G\) is a constant, \(r\) is the distance from an object to the center of the planet. The average force is then

\[ F=-\frac{\Delta G}{\Delta r} = -\frac{U(r+h)-U(r)}{h} = -\frac{1}{h}\left ( -\frac{G}{r+h} - \left(-\frac{G}{r} \right) \right) \]

\[ -\frac{1}{h}\left ( -\frac{G}{r+h} +\frac{G}{r} \right) = -\frac{1}{h}\left ( \frac{G(r+h)-Gr}{r(r+h)} \right) = \frac{1}{h}\left ( \frac{Gh}{r(r+h)} \right) = -\frac{G}{r(r+h)} \]

If \(h\approx 0, F\approx -\frac{G}{r^2}\).
