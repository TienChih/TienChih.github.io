\[-\sin(t) < \frac{\cos(t)-\cos(c)}{t-c} < -\sin(c) \]

Let \(D(t)=\begin{cases} \frac{\cos(t)-\cos(c)}{t-c} & t\neq c \\ -\sin(c) & t=c \end{cases}\).

\[-\sin(t) < \frac{\cos(t)-\cos(c)}{t-c} < -\sin(c) \]

Let \(D(t)=\begin{cases} \frac{\cos(t)-\cos(c)}{t-c} & t\neq c \\ -\sin(c) & t=c \end{cases}\).

\(D(t)-D(c)=\begin{cases} \frac{\cos(t)-\cos(c)}{t-c} + \sin(c)& t\neq c \\ 0 & t=c \end{cases}\)

\[\sin(c)-\sin(t) \leq D(t)-D(c) \leq 0 \]

\[|D(t)-D(c) |\leq |\sin(t)-\sin(c)|\]

\(D(t)-D(c)\) is continuous, \(D(c)\) is a constant, so \(D(t)\) is continuous.
