The Product Rule

\[\frac{d}{dt}[f(t)g(t)]=f'(t)g(t)+f(t)g'(t).\]

Quotient Rule

\[\frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}\right]=\frac{g(t)f'(t)-f(t)g'(t)}{g(t)^2}. \]

Let \(Q(t)=\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}\) so \(f(t)=g(t)Q(t)\). Differentiate both sides.

\[\begin{array}{rcl} Q(t)g(t) & =& f(t)\\ \frac{d}{dt}[Q(t)g(t)] & =& f'(t)\\ Q'(t)g(t)+Q(t)g'(t) & =& f'(t)\\ Q'(t) & =& \frac{f'(t)-Q(t)g'(t)}{g(t)}\\ \frac{d}{dt}\left[ \frac{f(t)}{g(t)} \right]& =& \frac{f'(t)-\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}g'(t)}{g(t)}\\ & =& \frac{f'(t)\frac{g(t)}{g(t)}-\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}g'(t)}{g(t)}\\ & =& \frac{g(t)f'(t)-f(t)g'(t)}{g(t)^2}. \end{array}\]
