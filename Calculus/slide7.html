Suppose a particles velocity is \(v(t)=t^2\)m/s \(t\) seconds after we start measuring.

If \(t\) is in \([0,1]\) whats the lowest the particle's velocity could be? The highest? What about in \([1,3]\)?

If \(t\) is in \([0,1]\) whats the shortest distance the particle's velocity could have traveled? What about in \([1,3]\)? Call the sum of these distances \(\underline{S}\)

If \(t\) is in \([0,1]\) whats the farthest distance the particle's velocity could have traveled? What about in \([1,3]\)? Call the sum of these distances \(\overline{S}\)

What is \(\overline{S}-\underline{S}\)?

If \(v\) is a constant, then the change of a particles position in \(\Delta t\) seconds moving at \(v\)m/s is \[\Delta x = v\Delta t.\]

If \(\underline{v}\leq v \leq \overline{v}\) then the change of a particles position in \(\Delta t\) seconds moving at \(v\)m/s satisfies \[\underline{v}\Delta t\leq v\Delta t \leq \overline{v} \Delta t.\]

Consider \(v(t)=t^2\) m/s over \([0,1]\) seconds. Subdivide \([0,1]\) into 4 regions: \([t_{i-1}, t_{i}]\) where \(t_0=0, t_1=0.25, t_2=0.5, t_3=0.75, t_4=1.\)

Consider \(v(t)=t^2\) m/s over \([0,1]\) seconds. Subdivide \([0,1]\) into 4 regions: \([t_{i-1}, t_{i}]\) where \(t_0=0, t_1=0.25, t_2=0.5, t_3=0.75, t_4=1.\)

Which are the lowest velocities in each of the four regions?

(A): \(0,\frac{1}{4},\frac{1}{2},\frac{3}{4}\ \ \) (B): \(\frac{1}{4},\frac{1}{2},\frac{3}{4},1\)

(C): \(0^2,\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2,\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2,\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2\ \ \ \ \) (D): \(\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2,\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2,\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2, 1^2\)

Consider \(v(t)=t^2\) m/s over \([0,2]\) seconds. Subdivide \([0,2]\) into 4 regions: \([t_{i-1}, t_{i}]\) where \(t_0=0, t_1=0.5, t_2=1, t_3=1.5, t_4=2.\)

Which represents the sum of the lowest displacements in each region?

(A): \(\underline{S}=1\cdot \left(0^2+ \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2+ \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2+ \left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2\right)\ \ \ \ \) (B): \(\underline{S}=1\cdot \left(\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2+ \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2+ 1^2\right)\)

(C): \(\underline{S}=\frac{1}{4}\cdot \left(0^2+ \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2+ \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2+ \left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2\right)\ \ \ \ \) (D): \(\underline{S}=\frac{1}{4}\cdot \left(\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2+ \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2+ 1^2\right)\)

(A): \(\overline{S}-\underline{S}=\displaystyle \frac{1}{4}\left(4^2-1^2\right) \ \ \ \ \) (B): \(\overline{S}-\underline{S}=\displaystyle \frac{1}{4}\left(4-1\right)\)
