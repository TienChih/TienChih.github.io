Potato

A potato is taken out of boiling water, let \(H\) represent the temperature of the potato in degrees celsius and \(t\) minutes after removing from water.

We have a baseline assumption that \(H\geq 27\), and the potato cools in relation to it's temperature by way of \(\displaystyle \frac{dH}{dt}=\frac{27-H}{4}\) degrees c per minute, and that the potato is 97 degrees celsius coming out of the water.

Find the line tangent to the graph of \(H\) as a function of \(t\) and use this to predict the temperature of the potato in 3 imnutes.

Find \(\displaystyle \frac{d^2H}{dt^2}\) and use a concavity argument to show whether or not this estimation is an over or under estimation.
