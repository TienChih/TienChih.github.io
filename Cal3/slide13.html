Suppose \(f:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}\) has a critical point at the origin.

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} f(x,y)&\approx& p_2(x,y)=f(0,0)+f_x(0,0)x+f_y(0,0)y\\ && +\frac{1}{2}\left(f_{xx}(0,0)x^2+2f_{xy}(0,0)xy+f_{yy}(0,0)y^2 \right). \end{array}\]

So \[ \begin{array}{rcl} \Delta(f) &\approx& \frac{1}{2}\left(f_{xx}(0,0)(\Delta x)^2+2f_{xy}(0,0)(\Delta x \Delta y)+f_{yy}(0,0)(\Delta y)^2 \right)\\ &=& \frac{1}{2}[\Delta x, \Delta y] \left[ \begin{array}{cc} f_{xx}(0,0) & f_{xy}(0,0)\\ f_{yx}(0,0) & f_{yy}(0,0) \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{cc} \Delta x\\ \Delta y\\ \end{array} \right] \end{array} \]

The Hessian

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^2\). Then the Hessian of \(f\) is: \[Hf= \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_n} \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_n} \\ \end{array} \right] \]

Let \(\mathbf{h}=\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{a}\). The Hessian \(Hf(\mathbf{a})\) is positive definite if for any \(\mathbf{a}\): \[ \mathbf{h}^THf(\mathbf{a})\mathbf{h}>0. \]

  • \(f\) has a local minimum at \(\mathbf{a}\) if \(Hf(\mathbf{a})\) is positive definite.
  • \(f\) has a local maximum at \(\mathbf{a}\) if \(Hf(\mathbf{a})\) is negative definite.
  • Otherwise, \(f\) has a saddle point at \(\mathbf{a}\).

Given a Hessian \(Hf(\mathbf{a})\) the sequence of principal minors is: \[ \begin{array}{rcl} d_1 &=& f_{x_1x_1}(\mathbf{a})\\ d_2 &=& \left| \begin{array}{cc} f_{x_1x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_1x_2}(\mathbf{a})\\ f_{x_2x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_2x_2}(\mathbf{a})\\ \end{array} \right| \\ d_3 &=& \left| \begin{array}{ccc} f_{x_1x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_1x_2}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_1x_3}(\mathbf{a})\\ f_{x_2x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_2x_2}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_2x_3}(\mathbf{a})\\ f_{x_3x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_3x_2}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_3x_3}(\mathbf{a})\\ \end{array} \right| \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \end{array} \]

  • If \(\text{det} \left(Hf(\mathbf{a})\right)=0\), \(\mathbf{a}\) is degenerate.
  • If \(d_k>0\) for each \(k\), \(Hf(\mathbf{a})\) is positive definite (\(\mathbf{a}\) is a local min).
  • If \(d_k<0\) when \(k\) is odd but \(d_k>0\) when \(k\) is even, \(Hf(\mathbf{a})\) is positive negative (\(\mathbf{a}\) is a local max).
  • Otherwise, \(f\) has a saddle point at \(\mathbf{a}\).

Extreme Value Theorem

If \(f:[a,b]\to\mathbb{R}\) is continuous, then \(f\) achieves a global maximum and minimum.

If \(X\subseteq \mathbb{R}^n\) is closed and bounded, then it is compact.

If \(X\) is compact and \(f:X\to \mathbb{R}\) is continuous, then \(f\) achieves a global maximum and minimum.

If \(X=[a,b]\), candidiates for extrema are critical points and endpoints.

If \(X=[a,b]\), candidiates for extrema are critical points and endpoints.

LaGrange Multipliers

Then if \(f|_S\) has an extremum at \(\mathbf{x}_0\) such that \(\nabla g(\mathbf{x})\neq0\) then there is a scalar \(\lambda\) such that \[\nabla f(\mathbf{x})=\lambda \nabla g(\mathbf{x}).\]
