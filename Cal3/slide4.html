Given vectors \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\), if the angle between them is \(\theta\), what is the area of the parallelogram they span in terms of \(|\mathbf{u}|, |\mathbf{v}|, \theta\)?

The Cross product \(\times\)

Given \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\in \mathbb{R}^3\) then \(\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v}\) the vector whose magnitude is the area of the parallelogram formed by \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\) (\(\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|\sin(\theta)\) where \(\theta\) is the angle between \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}\) ), and whose direction is determined by the "right-hand rule".

Products of Basis Vectors

\[\mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{j} = \mathbf{k}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{k} = \mathbf{i}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{i} = \mathbf{j}.\]

Basic Rules

Given \(\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}\in \mathbb{R}^3, c\in \mathbf{R}.\)

  • \(\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v} = -(\mathbf{v}\times \mathbf{u}) .\)
  • \( \mathbf{u}\times (\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w}) = \mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v}+\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{w}.\)
  • \((\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v})\times\mathbf{w} = \mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{w} + \mathbf{v}\times \mathbf{w}.\)
  • \((c\mathbf{u})\times \mathbf{v} = c(\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{u}\times (c\mathbf{v}).\)

Use \[\mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{j} = \mathbf{k}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{k} = \mathbf{i}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{i} = \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{i} = -\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{j} = -\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{k} = -\mathbf{j}\] to compute:

  • \((3\mathbf{i}-2\mathbf{k})\times(-4\mathbf{j}+3\mathbf{k}).\)
  • \((-4\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j})\times(2\mathbf{i}+%\mathbf{k}).\)
  • \((\mathbf{i}+2\mathbf{j})\times(2\mathbf{i}-6\mathbf{j}).\)

Use \[\mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{j} = \mathbf{k}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{k} = \mathbf{i}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{i} = \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{i} = -\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{j} = -\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{k} = -\mathbf{j}\] to compute:

  • \((\mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{j})\times \mathbf{j}\).
  • \(\mathbf{i}\times (\mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{j})\).

Use \[\mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{j} = \mathbf{k}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{k} = \mathbf{i}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{i} = \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{i} = -\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}\times \mathbf{j} = -\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{k} = -\mathbf{j}\] to compute:

  • \((\mathbf{i}\times \mathbf{j})\times \mathbf{j}\).
  • \(\mathbf{i}\times (\mathbf{j}\times \mathbf{j})\).

Cross Products as Determinants

If \(\mathbf{u}=\langle u_1, u_2. u_3\rangle, \mathbf{v}=\langle v_1, v_2, v_3\rangle\), then another way to compute \(\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}\) is: \[\begin{array}{rcl} \mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v} & = & \left| \begin{array}{ccc} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j}& \mathbf{k} \\ u_1 & u_2& u_3 \\ v_1 & v_2& v_3 \end{array} \right| \\ & = & \mathbf{i}\left|\begin{array}{cc} u_2 & u_3 \\ v_2 & v_3\end{array} \right| -\mathbf{j}\left|\begin{array}{cc} u_1 & u_3 \\ v_1 & v_3\end{array} \right| +\mathbf{k}\left|\begin{array}{cc} u_1 & u_2 \\ v_1 & v_2\end{array} \right|\end{array}\]

Cross Products as Determinants

If \(\mathbf{u}=\langle u_1, u_2. u_3\rangle, \mathbf{v}=\langle v_1, v_2, v_3\rangle\), then another way to compute \(\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}\) is: \[\begin{array}{rcl} \mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v} & = & \left| \begin{array}{ccc} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j}& \mathbf{k} \\ u_1 & u_2& u_3 \\ v_1 & v_2& v_3 \end{array} \right| \\ & = & \mathbf{i}\left|\begin{array}{cc} u_2 & u_3 \\ v_2 & v_3\end{array} \right| -\mathbf{j}\left|\begin{array}{cc} u_1 & u_3 \\ v_1 & v_3\end{array} \right| +\mathbf{k}\left|\begin{array}{cc} u_1 & u_2 \\ v_1 & v_2\end{array} \right|\\ &=& (u_2v_3-u_3v_2)\mathbf{i}-(u_1v_3-u_3v_1)\mathbf{j}+(u_1v_2-u_2v_1)\mathbf{k}.\end{array}\]

Cross Products as Determinants

If \(\mathbf{u}=\langle u_1, u_2. u_3\rangle, \mathbf{v}=\langle v_1, v_2, v_3\rangle\), then another way to compute \(\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}\) is: \[\begin{array}{rcl} \mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v} &=& (u_2v_3-u_3v_2)\mathbf{i}-(u_1v_3-u_3v_1)\mathbf{j}+(u_1v_2-u_2v_1)\mathbf{k}.\end{array}\]
