The Integral

Let \(f\) be defined on the rectangle \(R=[a,b]\times [c,d]\). Let \(c_{i,j}\) be any point inside of the subrectangle \[R_{i,j}=[x_i, x_{i+1}]\times[y_j, y_{j+1}], i,j=1,\ldots,n.\] Then the quantity \[S=\sum_{i,j=1}^n f(x_{i,j})\Delta A_{i,j}\] ( where \(\Delta A_{i,j}=\Delta x_i \Delta y_j=\text{area}(R_{i,j})\) ) is called a Riemann Sum of \(f\) on \(R\).

The double integral of \(f\) over \(R\) denoted \(\iint_R f dA\) is defined to be the limit of the Riemann sum over \(R\) as \(\Delta x, \Delta y\to 0\): \[\iint_R f dA:=\lim_{\Delta x, \Delta y\to 0}\left(\sum_{i,j=1}^n f(x_{i,j})\Delta A_{i,j}\right).\]

The double integral of \(f\) over \(R\) denoted \(\iint_R f dA\) is defined to be the limit of the Riemann sum over \(R\) as \(\Delta x, \Delta y\to 0\): \[\iint_R f dA:=\lim_{\Delta x, \Delta y\to 0}\left(\sum_{i,j=1}^n f(x_{i,j})\Delta A_{i,j}\right).\]

If \(f\) is continuous, or the set of discontinuities of \(f\) on \(R\) has 0 area, the integral exists.

Fubini's Theorem

If \(f\) is continuous, or the set of discontinuities of \(f\) on \(R\) has 0 area, and each horizontal or vertical line has at most finitely many discontinuities, then: \[\iint_R f dA=\int_a^b\int_c^df(x,y)dydx=\int_c^d\int_a^b f(x,y)dxdy.\]
