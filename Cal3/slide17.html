Greene's Theorem

Let \(R\subseteq \mathbb{R}^2\) be a closed and bounded region where it's boundary \(\partial R\) consists of finitely many simple closed, piecewise \(C^1\) curves. Orient \(\partial R\) so that \(R\) lies on the left of \(\partial R\).

Then for a vector field \(F(x,y)=(F_1(x,y), F_2(x,y))\) we have: \[\displaystyle \oint_{\partial R} F_1(x,y)dx+F_2(x,y)dy = \iint_R \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial x}\, dA.\]

Divergence Theorem

Let \(R\subseteq \mathbb{R}^2\) be a region with boundary \(\partial R\) as in Greens Theorem.

Then for each point on \(\partial R\), let \(\mathbf{n}\) denote the unit normal vector facing outward. Then the contour integral of \(F\cdot \mathbf{n}\) along \(\partial R\) is equal to the integral of the divergence of \(F\) over \(R\). Or: \[\displaystyle \oint_{\partial R} F\cdot \mathbf{n} ds = \iint_R \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial y}\, dA.\]
