How can we describe the instantaneous rate of change of \(\mathbf{x}(t)\)?

Given \(\mathbf{x}(t)=(x_1(t),\ldots, x_n(t))\), we have it's velocity: \[v(t)=\mathbf{x}'(t)=\lim_{\Delta t}\frac{\mathbf{x}(t+\Delta t)-\mathbf{x}(t)}{\Delta t}(x'_1(t),\ldots, x'_n(t)).\]

The speed of \(\mathbf{x}(t)\) is \(\|\mathbf{v}(t)\|\).

Arc-Length

Which of the following best approximates the arclength?

  • \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \Delta x_i+\Delta y_i\).
  • \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n (\Delta x_i)^2+(\Delta y_i)^2\).
  • \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{\Delta x_i+\Delta y_i}\).
  • \(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(\Delta x_i)^2+(\Delta y_i)^2}\).

\[\begin{array}{rcl} L & \approx & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(\Delta x_i)^2+(\Delta y_i)^2} \\ &= & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x(t_{i})-x(t_{i-1}))^2+(y(t_i)-y(t_{i-1}))^2} \\ &= & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x'(t_i^*)\Delta t)^2+(y'(t_i^{**})\Delta t)^2} \\ &= & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x'(t_i^*))^2+(y'(t_i^{**}))^2} \Delta t \\ \end{array}\]

\[\begin{array}{rcl} L & \approx & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(\Delta x_i)^2+(\Delta y_i)^2} \\ &= & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x(t_{i})-x(t_{i-1}))^2+(y(t_i)-y(t_{i-1}))^2} \\ &= & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x'(t_i^*)\Delta t)^2+(y'(t_i^{**})\Delta t)^2} \\ &= & \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x'(t_i^*))^2+(y'(t_i^{**}))^2} \Delta t \\ L&= & \displaystyle\lim_{\Delta t\to 0} \left(\displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{(x'(t_i^*))^2+(y'(t_i^{**}))^2} \Delta t \right)\\ &= & \displaystyle \int_a^b \sqrt{x'(t)^2+y'(t)^2}dt. \end{array}\]

The arclength from \(t=a\) to \(t=b\) of \(\mathbf{x}:\mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}^m\) can be approximated with: \[L\approx \displaystyle \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{\sum_{j=1}^m (\Delta (x_j)_i)^2}\] and computed: \[L=\int_a^b \sqrt{\sum_{j=1}^n x_j'(t)^2} dt.\]

Consider \(\mathbf{r}(t)=(5-t^2, 2+2t^2,2t^2-2 )\) and \(s=\int_0^{t_0}\|\mathbf{r}'(t)\|dt\) be it's arclength.

Find \(s\).

Solve \(t_0\) in terms of \(s\).

Find a parametric equation for the curve \(\mathbf{r}\) using \(s\) as a parameter.

\[s(t)=\int_a^t\|\mathbf{r}'(\tau)\| d\tau.\]

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \frac{ds}{dt}&=&\frac{d}{dt} \left[ \int_a^t\|\mathbf{r}'(\tau)\| d\tau\right]=\|\mathbf{r}'(t)\|.\\ \mathbf{r}'(t)&=&\mathbf{r}'(s)\frac{ds}{dt}\\ &=&\mathbf{r}'(s)\|\mathbf{r}'(t)\|.\\ \mathbf{r}'(s)&=&\frac{\mathbf{r}'(t)}{\|\mathbf{r}'(t)\|}.\\ \end{array} \]
