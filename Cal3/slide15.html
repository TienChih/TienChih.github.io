Change of Variables: Intro

Suppose we have a region \(D\subseteq \mathbb{R}^2\) over which we integrate \(f(u,v)\). Then suppose we have a transformation \(T:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}^2\) mapping \((u,v)\mapsto(x,y)\).

How can we get that: \[\int_Df(u,v)\,dudv = \int_{T(D)}???\,dxdy?\]

Consider the unit square \(D=[0,1]\times[0,1]\) (in \(uv\) coordinates). Describe the image \(T(D)\) in the \(xy\)-plane.

What is the area of \(T(D\))? (Hint: How might a cross-product be useful here?)

Change of Variables: General

Suppose we have a region \(D\subseteq \mathbb{R}^2\) over which we integrate \(f(u,v)\). Then suppose we have a differentiable transformation \(T:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}^2\).

Let \(D\) be the rectangle \(D=[u_0, u_0+d_u]\times[v_0\times t_v]\).

If \(T(u_0, v_0)=(x_0, y_0)\) Use local linear approximation to estimate \[T(u_0+du, v_0), T(u_0+du, v_0+dv), T(u_0, v_0+dv)\] in terms of \(\frac{dx}{du}, \frac{dx}{dv}, \frac{dy}{du}, \frac{dy}{dv}.\)

Estimate the area of \(T(D)\) using the corner points found above.

Let \(R:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}^2\) where \(T(u,v)=T(x(u,v), y(u,v))\), and \(T\) is differentiable.

The Jacobian of \(T\) is: \[\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}:=\text{det}DT(u,v)=\text{det}\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial v} \\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} \end{bmatrix} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} -\frac{\partial x}{\partial v} \frac{\partial y}{\partial u}.\] This measures the "size" of the transformation \(T\) at each point.

Let \(R:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}^2\) where \(T(u,v)=T(x(u,v), y(u,v))\), and \(T\) is differentiable.

If \(D, D^*\) are elementary regions of the \(xy, uv\) planes respectively such that \(T(D^*)=D\) in a bijective fashion, and \(f:D\to \mathbb{R}\) is integrabale over \(D\), we have: \[\iint_D f(x,y) dxdy=\iint_{D^*} f(x(u,v), y(u,v)) \left| \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}\right| dudv.\]
