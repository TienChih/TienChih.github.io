Given a vector field \(F:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}^n\), the divergence of \(F\) is denoted and defined to be: \[\text{div}(F)=\nabla\cdot F =\sum_{i=1}^n \frac{\partial F_i}{\partial x_i}\] which is a function \(\nabla\cdot F:\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}\)

This measure the "net mass flow" of each point.

Curl

What about 3 dimensions?

Let \(F=(F_1, F_2, F_3)\).

Circulation density about \(\mathbf{k}\) is \(\frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y}\).

Circulation density about \(\mathbf{i}\) is \(\frac{\partial F_3}{\partial y}-\frac{\partial F_2}{\partial z}\).

Circulation density about \(\mathbf{j}\) is \(\frac{\partial F_1}{\partial z}-\frac{\partial F_3}{\partial x}\).

The curl of \(F\) is a function which measures the vector around which \(F\) "swirls counter-clockwise" at each point.

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \text{curl}(F) =\nabla\times F &=& (\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\mathbf{i} + \frac{\partial}{\partial j}\mathbf{j} + \frac{\partial}{\partial z}\mathbf{k}) \times(F_1\mathbf{i} + F_2\mathbf{j} + F_3\mathbf{k}).\\ &=& \left| \begin{array}{ccc} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{j}\\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z}\\ F_1 & F_2 &F_3\\ \end{array} \right|\\ &=& (\frac{\partial F_3}{\partial y}-\frac{\partial F_2}{\partial z})\mathbf{i} + (\frac{\partial F_1}{\partial z}-\frac{\partial F_3}{\partial x})\mathbf{j} + (\frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y})\mathbf{k}. \end{array} \]
