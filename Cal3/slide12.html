Taylor's Theorem (Calc 2 Version)

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^k\). Then for \(a\in X\): \[p_k(x)=f(a)+f'(a)(x-a)+\frac{f''(a)}{2}(x-k)^2+\cdots+\frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}(x-a)^k\] is a "good approximation" of \(f\).

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^k\). Then for \(a\in X\): \[p_k(x)=f(a)+f'(a)(x-a)+\frac{f''(a)}{2}(x-k)^2+\cdots+\frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}(x-a)^k\] if we define \(R_k(x,a)=f(x)-p_k(x)\), we have \[\lim_{x\to a}\frac{R_k(x,a)}{(x-a)^k}=0.\]

Taylor's Theorem (Multivariable, First-Order)

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^1\). Then for \(\mathbf{a}\in X\): \[p_1(\mathbf{x})=f(\mathbf{a})+Df(\mathbf{a})(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{a})\] is a "good approximation" of \(f\). Meaning if \(R_1(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{a})=f(\mathbf{x})-p_1(\mathbf{x})\), we have \[\lim_{\mathbf{x}\to \mathbf{a}}\frac{R_1(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{a})}{\|(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{a})\|}=0.\]

Differentials

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}\). The incremental change of \(f\) is \[\Delta f =f(\mathbf{a}+\mathbf{h})-f(\mathbf{a}).\] The total differential is \[df(\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{h})=\sum_{i=1}^n \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i}(\mathbf{a})h_i.\]

Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}\) be defined by \(f(x,y)=x-y+2x^2+xy^2.\) Let \(\mathbf{a}=(2,-1), \mathbf{h}=(\Delta x, \Delta y)\).

Find \(\Delta f\), \(df((2,-1) , (\Delta x, \Delta y))\) and \(\Delta f-df((2,-1) , (\Delta x, \Delta y))\).

What is \(\Delta f-df((2,-1) , (\Delta x, \Delta y))\) if \(\Delta x = \Delta y = 0.01\)?

Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^2\). What should \(A,B,C,D,E, F\) be so that if \(p_2(x,y)=A+Bx+Cy+Dx^2+Exy+Fy^2\), we have:

  • \(f(0,0)=p_2(0,0)\).
  • \(f(x,y), p_2(y,y)\) have the same first derivatives at \((0,0)\).
  • \(f(x,y), p_2(y,y)\) have the same second derivatives at \((0,0)\).

Taylor's Theorem (Multivariable, Second-Order)

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^2\). Then for \(\mathbf{a}\in X\): \[p_2(\mathbf{x})=f(\mathbf{a})+\sum_{i=1}^n f_{x_i}(\mathbf{a})(x_i-a_i) + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i, j=1}^n f_{x_ix_j}(\mathbf{a})(x_i-a_i)(x_j-a_j)\] is a "good approximation" of \(f\). Meaning if \(R_2(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{a})=f(\mathbf{x})-p_2(\mathbf{x})\), we have \[\lim_{\mathbf{x}\to \mathbf{a}}\frac{R_2(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{a})}{\|(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{a})^2\|}=0.\]

Let \(f(x,y)=\cos(x)\cos(y).\) Find \(p_2(x,y)\).

The Hessian

Let \(f:X\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n\to \mathbb{R}\) be \(C^2\). Then the Hessian of \(f\) is: \[Hf= \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_n} \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_n} \\ \end{array} \right] \]

\[ p_2(\mathbf{x})=f(\mathbf{a})+\sum_{i=1}^n f_{x_i}(\mathbf{a})(x_i-a_i) + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i, j=1}^n f_{x_ix_j}(\mathbf{a})(x_i-a_i)(x_j-a_j) \]

Let \(\mathbf{h}=\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{a}\).

\[ p_2(\mathbf{x})=f(\mathbf{a}) + \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} f_{x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_2}(\mathbf{a}) & \cdots & f_{x_n}(\mathbf{a}) \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} h_1\\h_2\\ \vdots \\ h_n \end{array} \right] + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i, j=1}^n f_{x_ix_j}(\mathbf{a})(x_i-a_i)(x_j-a_j) \]

Let \(\mathbf{h}=\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{a}\).

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} p_2(\mathbf{x})&=&f(\mathbf{a}) + \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} f_{x_1}(\mathbf{a}) & f_{x_2}(\mathbf{a}) & \cdots & f_{x_n}(\mathbf{a}) \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} h_1\\h_2\\ \vdots \\ h_n \end{array} \right]\\ & & + \frac{1}{2} \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} h_1 & h_2 & \cdots & h_n \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_1 \partial x_n} \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_2 \partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_2} & \cdots &\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_n \partial x_n} \\ \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} h_1\\h_2\\ \vdots \\ h_n \end{array} \right] \end{array} \]

\[ f(\mathbf{a})+ Df(\mathbf{a})\mathbf{h} + \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{h}^THf(\mathbf{a})\mathbf{h}. \]
