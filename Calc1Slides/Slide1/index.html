What is the connection between these slopes and what you found before?

Given a function \(f(x)\), the average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over \([a,b]\) is \[m_{PQ} = \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\] which is also the slope of the secant line between points \(P=(a, f(a))\) and \(Q=(b, f(b))\).

What happens as \(c\) approaches 0?

Given a function \(f(x)\), the instantaneous rate of change or derivative of \(f(x)\) at \(a\) is the limit as \(h\) approaches 0 of the average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over \([a, a+h]\), or: \[f'(a):=\lim_{h\to 0} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{(a+h)-h}=\lim_{h\to 0} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\]

So...what's a limit?
