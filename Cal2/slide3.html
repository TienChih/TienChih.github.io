Intuition behind L'Hopitals

Consider \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) if \(f,g\) are differentiable, \(f(a), g(a)=0\) but \(g'(a)\neq 0\).

Consider \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) if \(f,g\) are differentiable, \(f(a), g(a)=0\) but \(g'(a)\neq 0\).

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}&=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-0}{g(x)-0}\\ \end{array} \]

Consider \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) if \(f,g\) are differentiable, \(f(a), g(a)=0\) but \(g'(a)\neq 0\).

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}&=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-0}{g(x)-0}\\ &=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{g(x)-g(a)}\\ \end{array} \]

Consider \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) if \(f,g\) are differentiable, \(f(a), g(a)=0\) but \(g'(a)\neq 0\).

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}&=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-0}{g(x)-0}\\ &=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{g(x)-g(a)}\\ &=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}}{\frac{g(x)-g(a)}{x-a}}\\ \end{array} \]

Consider \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) if \(f,g\) are differentiable, \(f(a), g(a)=0\) but \(g'(a)\neq 0\).

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}&=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-0}{g(x)-0}\\ &=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{g(x)-g(a)}\\ &=&\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}}{\frac{g(x)-g(a)}{x-a}}\\ &=&\displaystyle\frac{f'(a)}{g'(a)}=\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}\\ \end{array} \]

L'Hopitals Rule

Let \(f,g\) be differentiable functions such that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a}f(x)=\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a}g(x)=0\) or \(\infty\). Then \[\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\displaystyle \lim_{x\to a}\frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}.\] (If the limit even exists!)
