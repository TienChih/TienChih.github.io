Let \(\displaystyle \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\) be a rational function, where the degree of \(p\) is less than the degree of \(q\text{.}\)

  1. Linear Terms: Let \((x-a)^n\) divide \(q(x)\text{,}\) Then the decomposition of \(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\) will contain the terms \[ \begin{equation*} \frac{A_1}{(x-a)} + \frac{A_2}{(x-a)^2} + \cdots +\frac{A_n}{(x-a)^n}\text{.} \end{equation*} \]

  2. Quadratic Terms: Let \((x^2+bx+c)^n\) divide \(q(x)\text{,}\) where \(x^2+bx+c\) is irreducable. Then the decomposition of \(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\) will contain the terms \[ \begin{equation*} \frac{B_1x+C_1}{x^2+bx+c}+\frac{B_2x+C_2}{(x^2+bx+c)^2}+\cdots+\frac{B_nx+C_n}{(x^2+bx+c)^n}\text{.} \end{equation*}\]

Following is an example of a rather involved partial fraction decomposition.\[ \begin{align*} &\frac{7 \, x^{6} - 4 \, x^{5} + 41 \, x^{4} - 20 \, x^{3} + 24 \, x^{2} + 11 \, x + 16}{x(x-1)^2(x^2+4)^2}\\ =& \frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x-1}+\frac{C}{(x-1)^2}+\frac{Dx+E}{x^2+4}+\frac{Fx+G}{(x^2+4)^2} \end{align*} \]

Using some algebra, it's possible to find values for \(A\) through \(G\) to determine \[ \begin{align*} &\frac{7 \, x^{6} - 4 \, x^{5} + 41 \, x^{4} - 20 \, x^{3} + 24 \, x^{2} + 11 \, x + 16}{x(x-1)^2(x^2+4)^2}\\ =& \frac{1}{x}+\frac{2}{x-1}+\frac{3}{(x-1)^2}+\frac{4x+5}{x^2+4}+\frac{6x+7}{(x^2+4)^2}\text{.} \end{align*} \]
