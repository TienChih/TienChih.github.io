Sketch a function \(y=f(x)\) such that:

  • \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to1^-}f(x)\neq \lim_{x\to1^+}f(x)\) but neither limit is infinite.
  • \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to -3}f(x) \neq f(-3)\) but both are defined.
  • \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to0^-}f(x)\) is infinite but \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to0^+}f(x)\) is finite.
  • \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to \infty}f(x)=-\infty\) is infinite but \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to -\infty}f(x)\) is finite.

Find all limits to infinity, asymptotes and removable discontinuities of \[f(x)=\frac{(2x-6)(x+5)}{(x+2)(3-x)}\] and justify with the appropriate (left and right) limits.

Consider the function \[a(x) = \begin{cases} \sqrt{x + 7} - 3 & x < -3 \\ 0 & x = -3 \\ -x - 2 & -3 < x < 6 \\ -10 & x = 6 \\ -\frac{2 \, {\left(x - 7\right)}^{2}}{{\left| x - 7 \right|}} - 6 & x > 6 \end{cases}.\] Identify and classify all the discontinuities of \(a(x)\).

Use the limit formulation of the derivative to show whether or not the derivative of the function exists at \(c\). If not differentiable at \(c\), classify the derivative as a corner, vertical tangent, or vertical tangent with a cusp.

  • \( \displaystyle f(x) = {\left(x - 1\right)}^{2} {\left| x + 1 \right|}^{3}\) at \(c = -1\).
  • \( \displaystyle g(x) = {\left(x - 2\right)}^{2} {\left(x - 4\right)}^{\frac{2}{5}}\) at \(c = 4\).
